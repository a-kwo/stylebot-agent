Stage 0 — Occasion selection (1 screen, not a question) Multi-select, visual icons, takes 10 seconds. Sets context for everything after. Doesn't count toward your question budget.
Stage 1 — Energy + Cultural reference (4 questions) These two dimensions are deeply intertwined — someone's cultural reference point heavily predicts their energy level and vice versa. Test them together early because they narrow the space fastest.
Q1 — Primary: energy level. Show 3 full looks: one very quiet/understated, one middle, one expressive/loud. Forces a coarse energy calibration immediately.
Q2 — Primary: cultural reference. Show 4 looks representing sport/street, prep/institutional, European/fashion-forward, utility/workwear. Their pick immediately activates a sub-branch.
Q3 — Primary: cultural reference refinement. Based on Q2, show 3 looks within their chosen cultural space but varying in energy. Now you're cross-validating both dimensions.
Q4 — Primary: energy level in a social context. "Going out" scenario — three looks ranging from blends-in to stands-out. This also starts building your energy delta data passively.
Stage 2 — Silhouette (3 questions) Now that you know their general aesthetic territory, silhouette questions become much more precise.
Q5 — Primary: overall silhouette. Three looks, identical aesthetic/color, different fits — structured, relaxed, oversized. Isolates the variable cleanly.
Q6 — Primary: silhouette in a specific occasion context (pull their top occasion from Stage 0). Same principle, now grounded in their real life.
Q7 — Primary: top vs. bottom silhouette relationship. Some people like volume on top/slim on bottom, vice versa, or consistent throughout. This is a refinement question that adds resolution.
Stage 3 — Color relationship (2 questions) Q8 — Primary: color temperature and range. Show 4 looks: neutral monochrome, warm earth tones, cool/bold single color, mixed/patterned. Fast and decisive for most people.
Q9 — Primary: color expression level. Within their palette, how much do they use color as a statement vs. background? Three looks showing color as accent, as focal point, as full commitment.
Stage 4 — Occasion calibration (2-3 questions) These are targeted and quick — only asking about occasions they selected in Stage 0. If they selected three occasions you ask one question across them: "which of these three looks would you wear to [their occasion]?" You're not discovering new dimensions here, you're grounding the profile you've already built in practical context. This is also where you collect your energy delta data — if their picks shift dramatically between occasions, delta is high.
The output: After Stage 4 you have 5-7 data points per dimension, a primary cultural reference, and a silhouette/color profile. That's enough to generate a meaningful first recommendation set and a named aesthetic profile.
One implementation note for your friend: structure the scoring as a vector, not a label. Each answer updates a score across all four dimension axes. The final profile is a set of coordinates, not a category. That vector is what gets passed to your Claude API call as context — and it's also what gets more precise over time as users interact with recommendations. The quiz gives you a starting vector; behavior refines it.
